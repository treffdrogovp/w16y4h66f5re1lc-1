<p><p>The Gram Flour market is primarily categorized into two types: Desi and Kabuli. Desi Gram Flour is derived from smaller, darker seeds, known for its strong flavor and is commonly used in traditional dishes across South Asia. In contrast, Kabuli Gram Flour is made from larger, lighter-colored seeds, offering a milder taste and a finer texture, typically used in various international cuisines. These distinctions highlight cultural preferences and culinary applications, influencing market demand and pricing dynamics.</p></p>
